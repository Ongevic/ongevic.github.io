# Experimental Approaches to Grammaticality Judgments

## Overview

This project develops and applies experimental methods to investigate grammaticality judgments and language processing. We use psycholinguistic techniques to test linguistic theories and understand how speakers process and evaluate linguistic structures in real-time.

## Research Questions

- How do speakers make grammaticality judgments?
- What factors influence acceptability ratings?
- How do processing constraints affect grammaticality judgments?
- Can experimental methods provide insights into linguistic theory?

## Methodology

We employ a variety of experimental techniques:

1. **Acceptability Judgment Tasks**: Rating sentences for grammaticality
2. **Self-Paced Reading**: Measuring reading times for different sentence types
3. **Eye-Tracking**: Monitoring eye movements during sentence processing
4. **Event-Related Potentials (ERP)**: Measuring brain responses to linguistic stimuli
5. **Corpus Analysis**: Analyzing natural language data for frequency effects

## Key Findings

- Grammaticality judgments are influenced by multiple factors beyond pure grammaticality
- Processing difficulty affects acceptability ratings
- Individual differences in working memory impact judgment patterns
- Frequency effects play a significant role in acceptability
