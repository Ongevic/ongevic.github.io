# Acquisition of Complex Syntactic Structures

## Overview

This project investigates how children acquire complex syntactic structures during language development. We focus on understanding the cognitive mechanisms that enable children to learn and process complex grammatical constructions, including embedded clauses, relative clauses, and wh-questions.

## Research Questions

- What is the developmental trajectory for complex syntactic structures?
- How do children's processing abilities change as they acquire more complex syntax?
- What role does input frequency play in the acquisition of complex structures?
- Are there individual differences in the rate and pattern of syntactic development?

## Methodology

Our research employs multiple experimental paradigms:

1. **Longitudinal Studies**: Following children's syntactic development over time
2. **Cross-Sectional Studies**: Comparing children of different ages
3. **Eye-Tracking Experiments**: Measuring real-time processing of complex sentences
4. **Production Studies**: Analyzing children's spontaneous speech
5. **Comprehension Studies**: Testing children's understanding of complex structures

## Key Findings

- Children show systematic patterns in their acquisition of complex syntax
- Processing speed and accuracy improve with age and experience
- Individual differences in working memory capacity predict syntactic development
- Input quality and quantity significantly influence acquisition rates
